=== FIFA WORLD CUP 2026 — METLIFE STADIUM VOLUNTEER STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURES (SOP) ===
Document: Official Volunteer & Staff Operations Manual
Venue: MetLife Stadium, East Rutherford, New Jersey, USA
Last Updated: July 2026

=== SECTION 1: EMERGENCY MEDICAL SOP ===
In case of medical emergencies (injury, medical collapse, fainting, heat stroke, or hazard):
1. Stay calm. Secure the immediate area and prevent crowd crush around the patient.
2. Report precise section number, row, and seat coordinates to the Organizer Dispatch Console on radio channel 1 immediately.
3. Do NOT move the patient unless there is immediate danger (fire, structural collapse).
4. Guide incoming EMT/medical personnel to the patient location using the nearest aisle.
5. First Aid Centers are located near Section 109 (Level 1) and Section 215 (Level 2). Both are staffed with licensed paramedics.
6. AED (Automated External Defibrillator) units are mounted at every gate entrance and every other section concourse pillar.
7. If a fan requests medication (aspirin, inhaler, EpiPen), direct them to the nearest First Aid Center. Volunteers are NOT authorized to administer medication.

=== SECTION 2: FIRE & EVACUATION SOP ===
In case of fire, heavy smoke, or structural emergency:
1. Activate the nearest fire alarm pull station (red boxes located at every stairwell entrance).
2. Signal immediate evacuation to the nearest open gate (Gate A, B, or D). Do NOT direct fans to Gate C if congested (check congestion status on your radio).
3. Avoid elevators during evacuation. Direct all fans to staircases or ramps.
4. Assembly Points: Lot E (north side), Lot J (east side), Lot L (south side). Count heads at assembly and report to your zone captain.
5. Fans with disabilities: Prioritize evacuation via Gate A (step-free exit) or the access elevators near Section 112 (only if confirmed safe by fire marshal).
6. Do NOT re-enter the stadium until the "all clear" signal is given by the Emergency Operations Center (EOC).

=== SECTION 3: ACCESSIBILITY & STEP-FREE NAVIGATION ===
The main accessible entry is Gate A (North Concourse), which offers:
- Zero-step entries with automatic sliding doors
- Low-slope ramps (1:12 gradient, ADA compliant)
- Access elevators to Concourse Level 1 and Level 2
Step-free wheelchair pathing is highlighted in GREEN on the venue map.
Accessibility shuttle/golf carts operate constantly between Lot E, Lot G, and Gate A during match days.
Access elevators near Section 112 are operational and staffed by a volunteer at all times.
Assist visually impaired guests by offering verbal directions and guiding them to Gate A or the nearest operational elevator.
Wheelchair seating areas: Sections 103, 111, 124, 134 (Level 1) and Sections 203, 215, 228 (Level 2).
Companion seats are provided adjacent to all wheelchair positions.
Service animals are welcome at all areas of the stadium.

=== SECTION 4: CROWD MANAGEMENT SOP ===
1. Monitor crowd density using the congestion color system: GREEN (clear), YELLOW (moderate), RED (heavy — immediate action required).
2. If a section reaches RED congestion status, close the section to new entrants and redirect fans to adjacent sections.
3. Gate C frequently reaches RED status. When Gate C queue exceeds 20 minutes, activate the diversion protocol: redirect fans to Gate A (2 min wait) or Gate D (3 min wait) via PA announcement and digital signage.
4. Prevent standing in aisles, stairways, and concourse chokepoints. Politely ask fans to move to their assigned seats.
5. During halftime, expect 40% of fans to move to concourses. Position extra volunteers at Sections 115-120 (main food court area) to manage queues.

=== SECTION 5: SUSPICIOUS ACTIVITY & SECURITY SOP ===
1. If you observe suspicious behavior (unattended bags, aggressive individuals, unauthorized filming, attempts to breach restricted areas):
   - Do NOT confront the individual directly.
   - Maintain visual contact from a safe distance (minimum 10 meters).
   - Report immediately on radio channel 2 with: location, description of person/item, nature of concern.
   - Security Response Team (SRT) will be dispatched within 2 minutes.
2. Unattended bags: Do NOT touch or move. Establish a 15-meter perimeter and notify SRT on channel 2.
3. Ejection protocol: Only Security personnel can eject fans. Volunteers assist by guiding ejected fans to the nearest exit and ensuring they leave the premises.

=== SECTION 6: WEATHER EMERGENCY SOP ===
1. Lightning Protocol: If lightning is detected within 8 miles, the PA system will announce a weather hold. Fans must remain in covered concourse areas. Volunteers clear all outdoor seating areas.
2. Extreme Heat Protocol (temp > 95°F / 35°C): Activate water misting stations (Sections 105, 118, 130). Distribute free water cups. Direct fans showing heat exhaustion symptoms to First Aid.
3. Rain Protocol: Distribute free ponchos at Gate A and Gate D. Ensure drainage grates on concourses are clear of debris.

=== SECTION 7: VOLUNTEER SHIFT INFORMATION ===
Shift Schedule:
- Morning Shift: 4 hours before kickoff to kickoff (setup, gate management, fan guidance).
- Match Shift: Kickoff to final whistle (crowd management, emergency response, concourse patrol).
- Evening Shift: Final whistle to 3 hours after (exit management, cleanup, lost & found collection).
Check-in Location: Volunteer Center, Lot P (northwest corner of stadium complex).
Uniform: Official FIFA Volunteer polo (provided), comfortable closed-toe shoes, lanyard with photo ID badge.
Meals: Free meal voucher for stadium food courts (one per shift). Water bottles provided at check-in.
Radio Protocol: Channel 1 = Medical/Emergency. Channel 2 = Security. Channel 3 = General Operations. Channel 4 = VIP/Hospitality.
